Solid

  Due to the dense packing and slow motion of the molecules that make up a solid, it has a distinct structure and volume. Table salt, sugar, diamonds, and many other minerals are examples of crystalline solids. Solids are frequently crystalline. When liquids or gases are chilled, solids can occasionally form; ice is an example of a cooled liquid that has solidified. At room temperature, solids such as wood, metal, and rock also fall under this category.

Examples of solids are the following:

  • Rock
  • Iron
  • Diamond
  • Gold
