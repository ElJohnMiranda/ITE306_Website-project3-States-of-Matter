Liquid

  Despite taking on the shape of its container, liquids have defined volumes. Liquids include things like water and oil. When a gas cools, it may liquefy, as water vapor does. As the gas's molecules lose energy and slow down, this happens. Heat-induced liquefaction of solids is a possibility; molten lava is an example of a solid rock that has done so.

Examples of liquids are the following:

  • Water
  • Milk
  • Wine
  • Coffee
